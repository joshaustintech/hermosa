# Spanish Learning Curriculum

- `id`: `spanish-for-chicago-family-and-church`
- `version`: `1.0`
- `targetLearner`: English-speaking adult with linguistics/humanities background
- `goal`: Become conversational with Spanish-speaking extended relatives, especially in family, food, Chicago, church, and daily-life contexts

## Design Principles

- Teach sentence construction, not only memorized phrases.
- Introduce grammar through useful conversations.
- Use Chicago-local contexts to make lessons memorable.
- Include Protestant Christian vocabulary without assuming denominational uniformity.
- Favor high-frequency vocabulary and sentence patterns over exhaustive grammar.

## Parts of Speech Core

### Noun

- Spanish: `el sustantivo`
- Purpose: Naming people, places, things, and ideas.
- Examples: `la familia`, `el restaurante`, `la iglesia`, `la oración`

### Article

- Spanish: `el artículo`
- Purpose: Marking gender and number before nouns.
- Forms: `el`, `la`, `los`, `las`, `un`, `una`, `unos`, `unas`

### Pronoun

- Spanish: `el pronombre`
- Purpose: Replacing nouns and showing who is acting.
- Forms: `yo`, `tú`, `usted`, `él`, `ella`, `nosotros`, `ustedes`, `ellos`

### Verb

- Spanish: `el verbo`
- Purpose: Showing action, state, desire, movement, speech, and belief.
- Core verbs: `ser`, `estar`, `tener`, `hacer`, `ir`, `venir`, `querer`, `poder`, `deber`, `gustar`, `comer`, `hablar`, `vivir`, `creer`, `orar`, `leer`

### Adjective

- Spanish: `el adjetivo`
- Purpose: Describing nouns and agreeing in gender and number.
- Examples: `bueno / buena`, `grande`, `pequeño / pequeña`, `cansado / cansada`, `cristiano / cristiana`

### Adverb

- Spanish: `el adverbio`
- Purpose: Modifying verbs, adjectives, or whole sentences.
- Examples: `bien`, `mal`, `rápido`, `despacio`, `hoy`, `mañana`, `siempre`

### Preposition

- Spanish: `la preposición`
- Purpose: Showing relation, location, direction, or possession.
- Examples: `en`, `a`, `de`, `con`, `para`, `por`, `sin`

### Conjunction

- Spanish: `la conjunción`
- Purpose: Connecting words, phrases, and clauses.
- Examples: `y`, `pero`, `porque`, `si`, `cuando`

### Interjection

- Spanish: `la interjección`
- Purpose: Short emotional or conversational expressions.
- Examples: `¡Hola!`, `¡Claro!`, `¡Qué bien!`, `¡Ay!`

## Milestones

### M1: Survival Conversation

- I can greet relatives, introduce myself, ask simple questions, and say what I need.

### M2: Family and Food Conversation

- I can talk about family, meals, preferences, and simple plans.

### M3: Chicago Daily Life

- I can talk about neighborhoods, weather, transit, restaurants, and baseball.

### M4: Church and Faith Conversation

- I can understand and use common Protestant Christian vocabulary.

### M5: Conversational Confidence

- I can tell short stories, describe past events, make future plans, and ask follow-up questions.

## Lessons

### L01: Greetings, Courtesy, and Introductions

- Level: beginner
- Estimated minutes: 20
- Context: Meeting extended relatives at a family gathering.
- Grammar focus: Subject pronouns; formal vs informal address (`tú` and `usted`); basic sentence pattern `subject + verb + complement`.
- Vocabulary:
  - `hola` - hello
  - `buenos días` - good morning
  - `buenas tardes` - good afternoon
  - `buenas noches` - good evening / good night
  - `por favor` - please
  - `gracias` - thank you
  - `de nada` - you are welcome
  - `perdón` - excuse me / sorry
  - `me llamo` - my name is
  - `mucho gusto` - nice to meet you
- Model sentences:
  - `Hola, me llamo Josh.` - Hello, my name is Josh.
  - `Mucho gusto.` - Nice to meet you.
  - `¿Cómo se llama usted?` - What is your name?
  - `Gracias por recibirme.` - Thank you for welcoming me.
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: What does `mucho gusto` mean? Answer: `Nice to meet you`
  - Multiple choice: Complete `Hola, me ____ Josh.` Answer: `llamo`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish word means `Thank you`? Answer: `Gracias.`

### L02: Family Members and Relationships

- Level: beginner
- Estimated minutes: 25
- Context: Talking with extended relatives about family.
- Grammar focus: Nouns and gender; definite articles `el`, `la`, `los`, `las`; possession with `mi`, `tu`, `su`, `nuestro`.
- Vocabulary:
  - `la familia` - family
  - `el padre` - father
  - `la madre` - mother
  - `el esposo` - husband
  - `la esposa` - wife
  - `el hijo` - son
  - `la hija` - daughter
  - `el tío` - uncle
  - `la tía` - aunt
  - `el primo` - male cousin
  - `la prima` - female cousin
  - `los abuelos` - grandparents
- Model sentences:
  - `Esta es mi esposa.` - This is my wife.
  - `Mi hija se llama Summer.` - My daughter is named Summer.
  - `¿Cuántos hermanos tiene usted?` - How many siblings do you have?
  - `Tengo una familia grande.` - I have a large family.
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: Which word means `daughter`? Answer: `la hija`
  - Multiple choice: Complete `Esta es mi ____.` Answer: `esposa`
  - Multiple choice: What part of speech is `familia`? Answer: `noun`

### L03: Ser, Estar, and Basic Descriptions

- Level: beginner
- Estimated minutes: 30
- Context: Describing people, places, and temporary states.
- Grammar focus: `ser` for identity and permanent traits; `estar` for location and temporary states; adjective agreement.
- Vocabulary:
  - `soy` - I am
  - `eres` - you are
  - `es` - he/she/it is
  - `estoy` - I am
  - `estás` - you are
  - `está` - he/she/it is
  - `cansado` - tired
  - `contento` - happy
  - `ocupado` - busy
  - `en Chicago` - in Chicago
- Model sentences:
  - `Soy de Chicago.` - I am from Chicago.
  - `Estoy en la casa.` - I am at the house.
  - `Mi hija está contenta.` - My daughter is happy.
  - `La iglesia es pequeña.` - The church is small.
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: Choose the best verb: `Yo ____ de Chicago.` Answer: `soy`
  - Multiple choice: Choose the best preposition: `Estoy ____ la iglesia.` Answer: `en`

### L04: Food, Preferences, and Ordering

- Level: beginner
- Estimated minutes: 30
- Context: Eating with relatives or talking about Chicago food.
- Grammar focus: `gustar` pattern (`me gusta` / `me gustan`); direct objects in simple food sentences; polite requests with `quiero` and `quisiera`.
- Vocabulary:
  - `la comida` - food
  - `el agua` - water
  - `el café` - coffee
  - `el pan` - bread
  - `la carne` - meat
  - `el pollo` - chicken
  - `el arroz` - rice
  - `los frijoles` - beans
  - `me gusta` - I like
  - `quisiera` - I would like
- Chicago localization:
  - Talking about eating a hot dog after a Cubs game.
  - Ordering food with relatives near a Chicago neighborhood restaurant.
- Model sentences:
  - `Me gusta la comida mexicana.` - I like Mexican food.
  - `Quisiera agua, por favor.` - I would like water, please.
  - `Después del partido de los Cubs, quiero comer.` - After the Cubs game, I want to eat.
  - `¿Qué le gusta comer?` - What do you like to eat?
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: What does `quisiera` mean? Answer: `I would like`
  - Multiple choice: Complete `Me ____ la comida mexicana.` Answer: `gusta`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `I would like coffee, please.`? Answer: `Quisiera café, por favor.`

### L05: Present-Tense Verbs for Conversation

- Level: beginner
- Estimated minutes: 35
- Context: Talking about daily life and habits.
- Grammar focus: `-ar`, `-er`, and `-ir` present-tense patterns; verb stems and endings; negation with `no`.
- Vocabulary:

  - `vivir` - to live
  - `trabajar` - to work
  - `aprender` - to learn
  - `escribir` - to write

  - `escuchar` - to listen
- Model sentences:

  - `Vivo en Chicago.` - I live in Chicago.

- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: Which form is `yo` for `hablar`? Answer: `hablo`
  - Multiple choice: Which form is `yo` for `vivir`? Answer: `vivo`
  - Multiple choice: Complete `No ____ todo.` Answer: `entiendo`

### L07: Chicago, Neighborhoods, Weather, and Transit

- Level: beginner-intermediate
- Estimated minutes: 35
- Context: Talking about life in Chicago.

- Vocabulary:
  - `la ciudad` - city
  - `el barrio` - neighborhood
  - `el tren` - train
  - `el autobús` - bus
  - `el lago` - lake
  - `el invierno` - winter
  - `hace frío` - it is cold
  - `hace calor` - it is hot
  - `hay nieve` - there is snow
  - `cerca de` - near
  - `lejos de` - far from
- Chicago localization:
  - Talking about Lake Michigan.
  - Talking about taking the train to a Cubs game.
  - Talking about Chicago winter weather.
- Model sentences:
  - `Chicago es una ciudad grande.` - Chicago is a large city.
  - `Vivo cerca del lago.` - I live near the lake.
  - `Hace frío en el invierno.` - It is cold in the winter.

- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: What does `hay nieve` mean? Answer: `there is snow`
  - Multiple choice: Complete `Vivo cerca ____ lago.` Answer: `del`

### L08: Church, Worship, and Protestant Vocabulary

- Level: beginner-intermediate
- Estimated minutes: 40
- Context: Attending church or talking with Spanish-speaking Protestant Christians.
- Grammar focus: Religious nouns; using `creer`, `orar`, `leer`, `cantar`, `predicar`; talking respectfully about belief.
- Vocabulary:
  - `Dios` - God
  - `Jesucristo` - Jesus Christ
  - `el Espíritu Santo` - the Holy Spirit
  - `la Biblia` - the Bible
  - `la iglesia` - church
  - `el culto` - worship service
  - `la oración` - prayer
  - `orar` - to pray
  - `el sermón` - sermon
  - `el pastor` - pastor
  - `la gracia` - grace
  - `la fe` - faith
  - `el pecado` - sin
  - `el perdón` - forgiveness
  - `la salvación` - salvation
  - `el evangelio` - the gospel
  - `la alabanza` - praise/worship music
  - `el himno` - hymn
- Model sentences:
  - `Voy a la iglesia los domingos.` - I go to church on Sundays.

  - `¿Puedo orar por usted?` - May I pray for you?

- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: What does `el sermón` mean? Answer: `sermon`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `May I pray for you?`? Answer: `¿Puedo orar por usted?`
  - Multiple choice: What part of speech is `orar`? Answer: `verb`

### L09: Past Events: What Happened?

- Level: intermediate
- Estimated minutes: 45
- Context: Talking about what happened yesterday, last weekend, or at church.
- Grammar focus: Preterite tense for completed actions; common irregular preterites (`fui`, `tuve`, `hice`, `vine`); time markers `ayer`, `anoche`, `la semana pasada`.
- Vocabulary:
  - `ayer` - yesterday
  - `anoche` - last night
  - `la semana pasada` - last week
  - `fui` - I went / I was
  - `tuve` - I had
  - `hice` - I did / I made
  - `vine` - I came
  - `comí` - I ate
  - `hablé` - I spoke
- Model sentences:
  - `Ayer fui a la iglesia.` - Yesterday I went to church.
  - `Anoche comí con mi familia.` - Last night I ate with my family.
  - `Hablé con mi tío después del culto.` - I spoke with my uncle after the worship service.
  - `La semana pasada fuimos a un partido de los Cubs.` - Last week we went to a Cubs game.
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: What does `ayer` mean? Answer: `yesterday`
  - Multiple choice: Which form is the `yo` preterite of `hablar`? Answer: `hablé`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `Yesterday I went to church.`? Answer: `Ayer fui a la iglesia.`

- Quiz:

### L11: Conversation Repair and Clarification

- Level: intermediate
- Estimated minutes: 35
- Context: Handling real conversations when you miss something.
- Grammar focus: Useful meta-language; requests for repetition; asking for slower speech.
- Vocabulary:
  - `No entiendo` - I do not understand
  - `Entiendo un poco` - I understand a little
  - `¿Puede repetir?` - Can you repeat?
  - `Más despacio, por favor` - More slowly, please
  - `¿Qué significa?` - What does it mean?
  - `¿Cómo se dice?` - How do you say?
  - `Estoy aprendiendo español` - I am learning Spanish
- Model sentences:
  - `Estoy aprendiendo español. ¿Puede repetir?` - I am learning Spanish. Can you repeat?
  - `Más despacio, por favor.` - More slowly, please.
  - `¿Qué significa esta palabra?` - What does this word mean?
  - `Entiendo un poco, pero necesito ayuda.` - I understand a little, but I need help.
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `More slowly, please.`? Answer: `Más despacio, por favor.`
  - Multiple choice: What should you say when you need repetition? Answer: `¿Puede repetir?`

### L12: Capstone: Family, Chicago, and Church Conversation

- Level: intermediate
- Estimated minutes: 60
- Context: A realistic extended conversation with relatives.
- Grammar focus: Mixing present, past, and future; asking follow-up questions; using family, food, Chicago, and church vocabulary together.
- Scenario prompt: You are visiting relatives. You talk about family, Chicago, food, church, and plans for Sunday.
- Model dialogue:
  - Relative: `Hola, Josh. ¿Cómo estás?` - Hi, Josh. How are you?
  - Learner: `Estoy bien, gracias. Estoy aprendiendo español.` - I am well, thank you. I am learning Spanish.
  - Relative: `¡Qué bien! ¿Vives en Chicago?` - Great! Do you live in Chicago?
  - Learner: `Sí, vivo en Chicago. Hace frío en el invierno, pero me gusta la ciudad.` - Yes, I live in Chicago. It is cold in the winter, but I like the city.
  - Relative: `¿Qué te gusta comer?` - What do you like to eat?
  - Learner: `Me gusta la comida mexicana y también comer después de un partido de los Cubs.` - I like Mexican food and also eating after a Cubs game.
  - Relative: `¿Vas a la iglesia?` - Do you go to church?
  - Learner: `Sí, voy a la iglesia los domingos. Leemos la Biblia, cantamos himnos y escuchamos el sermón.` - Yes, I go to church on Sundays. We read the Bible, sing hymns, and listen to the sermon.
  - Relative: `Muy bien. ¿Quieres venir con nosotros el domingo?` - Very good. Do you want to come with us on Sunday?
  - Learner: `Claro, gracias. ¿A qué hora empieza el culto?` - Of course, thank you. What time does the worship service start?
- Quiz:
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `I live in Chicago and I am learning Spanish.`? Answer: `Vivo en Chicago y estoy aprendiendo español.`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish question means `What time does the worship service start?`? Answer: `¿A qué hora empieza el culto?`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `I like Mexican food.`? Answer: `Me gusta la comida mexicana.`
  - Multiple choice: Which Spanish sentence means `Can you repeat more slowly, please?` Answer: `¿Puede repetir más despacio, por favor?`

## Review System

Quiz variation guidance:

- Shuffle the order of answer options each time a quiz question is shown.
- Rotate incorrect answers between quiz attempts where suitable alternatives exist.
- Prefer broader distractor pools so repeated reviews test recognition rather than fixed-position memory.

### Daily Review

- 10 minutes
- Review 10 vocabulary words.
- Answer 5 grammar questions.
- Answer 3 tap-based comprehension questions.
- Review 1 model sentence silently.

### Spaced Repetition

- Same-day
- 1 day
- 3 days
- 7 days
- 14 days
- 30 days

## Conversational Minimum

- I can introduce myself.
- I can describe my family.
- I can ask simple questions.
- I can talk about food preferences.
- I can talk about Chicago and daily life.
- I can talk about church, worship, prayer, Scripture, and basic Christian belief.

- I can describe what happened recently.
